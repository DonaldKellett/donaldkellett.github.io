My Experiences in EPYMT

My EPYMT course "Towards Differential Geometry" started on the 16th of July, 2014. After that, I have to attend the course every Monday, Wednesday and Friday until 4th of August. The test will be held at 7th of August, and if I pass, I can get two "points" in CUHK.

The course is mainly about differential geometry (as the name suggests), which is a topic in mathematics that is studied in university. I chose to attend the course because I am a fan of calculus.

After the first few lessons, I found the course challenging but fun. This course gave me a rough idea of what mathematics at university level might be like. In the very first lesson, the professor told us that mathematics at university is quite different from mathematics in high school, because in university, students are asked to prove lots of theorems and formulae instead of doing calculations. The professor also said that he was certain not everyone would pass the course. He said that a few students lost interest in mathematics (in fact, they became quite afraid of mathematics) after taking the course. At last, he said that if we managed to pass the course, we are very clever already, which made us feel better.

Up until now, I do not find the course content too hard to understand. I believe that I will do okay in my exam.
