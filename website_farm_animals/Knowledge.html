Human civilization started with making houses and colonies. Then they began keeping some animals in order to fulfill their basic needs. This gave rise to animal husbandry and human beings started keeping animals to provide meat, milk, transportation, companionship and other benefits. This is the reason behind the huge farm animals list. Different farm animals fulfill different purposes; hence the list given in this article will help you to understand which animals are used for what purpose.

Different farm animals serve different purposes. They can be segregated into dairy animals, poultry animals, meat producing animals, and animals used for transportation. Dairy animals include cows, buffalo, goats and sheep. Cows and goats are now common across the world. Poultry animals such as chicken, turkey, ducks are always high in demand because of their ease of rearing, and are also known for various health benefits. Some farm animals like horses, donkeys, yaks, and llamas are used for transportation. Other farm animals like dogs and cats have special uses. Dogs protect the property, and can be trained to work with humans such as herding sheep. Cats catch rats and other pests that can destroy stored crops. There are many other animals also which are included in the farm animal list. Just have a look!

Cows: Cows are, and have always been used at farms. Though the main uses of cows are for milk and meat, they are also used for transportation in many parts of the world. Here are some of the major breeds of cows that are used for milk, and beef.

Chickens: A chicken is a kind of bird that is kept as a source of food. Chickens lay eggs, which we can eat, and we can also eat chickens. A female chicken is called a hen, male chickens are called roosters. Hens lay eggs, roosters’ crow when the sun rises.

Ducks: ducks are the best animals. They are one of the only animals that live on land, Water and air. Ducks are adorable especially baby duckling they are so fluffy.

Goats: Goats are closely related to sheep. People use goats for their milk and their meat, and some kinds of goat have wool that can be used to make clothes from. Goats’ milk can be made into cheese. Male goats are called bucks female goats are called does or nannies.

Pigs: pigs have large heads, short legs and a large body. They eat almost anything, and spend most of their time digging through the ground with their snout looking for food. A male pig is called a boar, a female pig is called a sow and a baby pig is called a piglet. Pigs are generally kept for their meat, which is called pork.
